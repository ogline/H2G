About the Calendars

Gregorian Calendar: The solar calendar used in most of the world, introduced by Pope Gregory XIII in 1582.

Hijri Calendar: The Islamic lunar calendar consisting of 12 months in a year of 354 or 355 days. It begins with the migration of Prophet Muhammad from Mecca to Medina.

Note: This converter uses approximate calculations. For precise religious dates, consult official Islamic authorities.
